Raining Cats and Dogs!

Name

"Raining Cats and Dogs"

High Concept

Cats and dogs are falling from the sky! You need to shoot down the cats but be carful not to hit a dog.

(I'll potentially change it from cats and dogs to something else as long as they're 2 different types of things falling down the screen)

Genre

Arcade

Platform

Desktop (probably simple enough to port to mobile if I ever want/need to)

Story

They're falling into your mom's house but shes allergic to cats or something. Or maybe just cats are evil and dogs are chill.

Astetics

Goofy over everything. There isn't going to really be any aniation outside of them maybe exploding.

Gameplay

The controls will be very simple. They will have a gun the swivels from the middle botton of the screen that points at their mouse and shoots when they click. Potentially instead of that, there it won't swivel and instead will always be pointing up but will match the X position of their mouse.

The basic challange will be a simple galega style arcade shooter of making sure they destroy all the cats. They can't just shoot wildly though because if they hit a dog then they loose (or maybe there is a life system, in which case I would maybe include health packs). Its a simple game of aim, reflexes, and restraint. The enemy spawn will increase over time and the player's goal will be to get a high score. I will record the high score using local storage.

To teach them I will maybe have text appear when the game starts or maybe just in the starting screen that says something along the lines of "Shoot down the cats but don't hit a dog!".

Proscess

I started out with the Circle Blast exercise as the basic framework for getting things to move and load properly. I then started to deviate heavily from that to make my own vision a reality.

The first step was getting the gun to work. I had to do some weird math to make sure it was always pointing in the right direction. I then also had to mess around with the bullets. It took me an emberrasingly long time to figure out that the "x" and "y" in the comstructor are where to render it relative to the center, not the actual X and Y coordinates of the object... I eventually figured that out though and then bullets shot from the end of the gun!

I then made the animals work. My original plan was to have different classes for cats and dogs, but I realized that they reused much of the code and the only things that changed were their sprites and the interactions when they got shot or reached the bottom of the screen, so instead, I made 1 "Animal" class and gave them bool values for if it is a cat or not and if it is an enemy or not. Being a cat (or dog) determined the sprite that gets loaded, and being an enemy determines its interactions. This way I could pretty easily change which animals were the enemies (which was a requested feature by multiple people).

I also added a feature where the game is able to detect and display the highscore of the user and its saved to local storage, so it persists when the user leaves the page.

That took most of the time. I also did some cleanup, such as adding a font, adding a favicon, adding sound effects, and adding an end screen.

Things To Look For

I am proud of loading in and saving the highscore from local storage, that is a good feature. It makes it feel more game-y and adds far more replayability since now you have a more clear goal.

Making the gun pivot from the center and follow the mouse as well as making the bullets spawn at the end of the gun was difficult to impliment but came out well.

Making the cats and dogs switchable did require that I change my entire plan, which was difficult but turned out well enough. Now there is a button on the start and end screen with updating text that shows which is the enemy. That was a highly requested feature that makes the game much more fun.

I did also make a way to randomly choose which sprite to use, adding some variety and humor to the game. Its a modular system so if I ever want to have more then I can easily add them in and change the "numOfSprites" constant. They've also all been compressed to not be too large of files.

I added a font but I think PIXI is finicky about loading it for the first time. I tried to use the fontFaceObserver library to load in the fonts before but the documentation made no sense to me.
